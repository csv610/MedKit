## **Step 1: Appearance and Behavior**

**Medical Objective:** Evaluate baseline psychomotor function, self-care, and level of consciousness. Detect signs of psychiatric or neurological disorders.

**Assessment:**

## **Step 3: Mood and Affect**

**Medical Objective:** Assess emotional state and congruence between affect and mood. Identify mood disorders.
**Rationale:** Mood and affect disturbances are core symptoms of depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and psychotic disorders.

**Assessment:**

* Ask about current emotional state and recent mood changes.
* Observe facial expression, range, intensity, and appropriateness of affect.

**Sample Questions / Prompts:**

* ‚ÄúHow have you been feeling lately?‚Äù
* ‚ÄúHave you noticed a change in what brings you joy or sadness?‚Äù

**Medical Significance:**

* Flat or blunted affect ‚Üí schizophrenia or depression.
* Labile affect ‚Üí bipolar disorder or borderline personality disorder.
* Dysphoria or anhedonia ‚Üí depression.

## **Step 4: Orientation (One of Five Core Tests)**

**Medical Objective:** Evaluate awareness of self, environment, and situation.
**Rationale:** Disorientation may indicate delirium, dementia, metabolic disorders, or acute psychiatric episodes.

**Assessment Questions:**

* ‚ÄúCan you tell me your full name and age?‚Äù
* ‚ÄúWhere are we now?‚Äù
* ‚ÄúWhat is the date and day of the week?‚Äù
* ‚ÄúWhy are you here today?‚Äù

**Medical Significance:**

* Disorientation to time ‚Üí early cognitive decline or delirium.
* Disorientation to place ‚Üí moderate/severe cognitive impairment.
* Disorientation to person ‚Üí severe neurocognitive disorder.

## **Step 5: Attention and Concentration (Second Core Test)**

**Medical Objective:** Evaluate working memory and ability to sustain mental effort.
**Rationale:** Impaired attention is common in delirium, ADHD, anxiety, depression, and frontal lobe disorders.

**Assessment Tasks:**

* Serial 7s subtraction from 100.
* Spell ‚ÄúWORLD‚Äù backward.
* Recite months in reverse order.

**Medical Significance:**

* Inability to perform ‚Üí frontal lobe dysfunction or delirium.

## **Step 6: Memory (Third Core Test)**

**Medical Objective:** Assess immediate, recent, and remote memory for cognitive integrity.
**Rationale:** Memory impairment is critical for detecting dementia, amnestic disorders, and brain lesions.

**Assessment:**

* Immediate recall: repeat 3 words (*apple, table, penny*).
* Recent memory: recall breakfast, yesterday‚Äôs events.
* Remote memory: childhood school, historical facts.

**Medical Significance:**

* Impaired recent memory ‚Üí early dementia, delirium, or head injury.
* Impaired remote memory ‚Üí severe neurodegenerative disorder or retrograde amnesia.

## **Step 7: Thought Process and Content**

**Medical Objective:** Detect disorders of thinking that may indicate psychosis, mood disorder, or cognitive dysfunction.
**Rationale:** Disordered thinking or abnormal content (delusions, obsessions) can guide diagnosis and risk assessment.

**Assessment:**

* Thought Process: linearity, coherence, flight of ideas, tangentiality.
* Thought Content: preoccupations, delusions, obsessions, suicidal or homicidal ideation.

**Sample Questions / Prompts:**

* ‚ÄúHave you felt that someone is trying to harm you?‚Äù
* ‚ÄúDo your thoughts ever feel blocked or racing?‚Äù

**Medical Significance:**

* Thought blocking ‚Üí schizophrenia.
* Grandiose delusions ‚Üí mania.
* Paranoid ideation ‚Üí psychotic disorder.

## **Step 8: Perception**

**Medical Objective:** Identify hallucinations, illusions, depersonalization, or derealization.
**Rationale:** Abnormal perceptions are hallmarks of psychotic disorders, delirium, or substance intoxication.

**Assessment Questions:**

* ‚ÄúHave you heard voices when no one is around?‚Äù

**Medical Significance:**

## **Step 9: Abstract Thinking (Fourth Core Test)**

**Medical Objective:** Assess conceptual reasoning, judgment, and executive function.
**Rationale:** Concrete thinking indicates frontal lobe pathology, intellectual disability, or psychosis.

**Assessment Questions / Tasks:**

* Interpret proverb: ‚ÄúA rolling stone gathers no moss.‚Äù
* Compare similarity of objects: ‚ÄúHow are an apple and orange alike?‚Äù

**Medical Significance:**

* Concrete responses ‚Üí frontal lobe lesion or schizophrenia.
* Poor abstraction ‚Üí dementia or intellectual disability.

## **Step 10: Judgment (Fifth Core Test)**

**Medical Objective:** Evaluate decision-making, problem-solving, and risk assessment.
**Rationale:** Impaired judgment is seen in frontal lobe damage, mania, substance use, or psychotic disorders.

**Assessment Questions:**

## **Step 11: Insight**

**Medical Objective:** Assess awareness of illness, treatment need, and self-understanding.
**Rationale:** Insight is crucial for compliance, prognosis, and risk management.
