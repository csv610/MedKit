ANE Child Interview (<10 years old)
1. Introduction and Consent

Purpose: Establish trust, explain the process, and get child assent with caregiver consent.

Questions:

“Hi, I’m here to help you feel safe and see if anything hurt you. Is it okay if we talk for a little while?”

“Do you want mommy, daddy, or someone you trust to sit with you while we talk?”

“I’m going to ask some questions and look at your body to see if you have any boo-boos. Is that okay?”

Typical Child Responses:

“Yes, I want mommy here.”

“I’m scared, but okay.”

“No, I don’t want anyone here.”

Normal Indications: Child agrees to participate, caregiver present, child is alert and oriented.
Abnormal Indications: Child refuses, very distressed, or seems coerced.

2. General Health

Purpose: Collect baseline medical information.

Questions:

“Do you take any medicine?”

“Do you have any allergies?”

“Have you felt sick or hurt anywhere recently?”

Typical Responses:

“I take medicine for my asthma.”

“I’m allergic to peanuts.”

“No, I feel okay.”

Normal: No acute illness or injuries.
Abnormal: Active illness, untreated injuries, severe allergy reactions.

3. Incident / Safety

Purpose: Understand what happened in simple, non-leading terms.

Questions:

“Can you tell me what happened that made you feel scared or hurt?”

“Where were you when this happened?”

“Who was there with you?”

“Did anyone use force or threaten you?”

“Did anyone touch you that you didn’t like?”

Typical Responses:

“He grabbed me at the park.”

“I was at home, and a man came in.”

“My friend was there.”

“He told me not to scream.”

“Yes, he touched my private parts.”

Normal: Child describes fear or minor incidents without sexual contact.
Abnormal: Child describes inappropriate sexual contact, threats, or physical restraint.

4. Body Touch / Sexual Contact

Purpose: Identify inappropriate contact age-appropriately.

Questions:

“Did anyone touch your private parts?”

“Did anyone make you touch their private parts?”

“Did anyone show you pictures or videos you didn’t like?”

“Did anyone try to take your clothes off?”

Typical Responses:

“Yes, he touched me under my clothes.”

“No, he didn’t make me touch him.”

“He showed me a bad picture.”

“He tried to pull my shirt off.”

Normal: No contact or only accidental contact.
Abnormal: Direct sexual contact, coercion, or exposure.

5. Pain and Injury

Purpose: Detect physical trauma or medical needs.

Questions:

“Does any part of your body hurt?”

“Do you have any boo-boos, bruises, or marks?”

“Does it hurt when you sit, walk, or go to the bathroom?”

“Do you feel dizzy, tired, or sick?”

Typical Responses:

“My arm hurts where he grabbed me.”

“I have a bruise on my leg.”

“It hurts when I pee.”

“I feel dizzy.”

Normal: No injuries, pain minimal.
Abnormal: Bruises, genital/anal trauma, bleeding, concussion signs.

6. Evidence / Clothing

Purpose: Preserve forensic evidence when relevant.

Questions:

“Are you wearing the same clothes you had when this happened?”

“Did you change or wash your clothes after?”

“Did you keep anything from that day?”

Typical Responses:

“Yes, same clothes.”

“I changed and washed them.”

“I kept the shirt.”

Normal: Clothing intact, minimal contamination.
Abnormal: Washed or discarded clothing, loss of potential evidence.

7. Emotional Assessment

Purpose: Understand emotional state and trauma.

Questions:

“How do you feel right now?”

“Are you scared to be at home or school?”

“Do you have someone to talk to when you are scared?”

“Do you feel sad or want to be alone a lot?”

Typical Responses:

“I’m scared.”

“I don’t want to go home.”

“I talk to my mom.”

“I just want to be alone.”

Normal: Mild fear, child has support.
Abnormal: Severe distress, unsafe living situation, no support.

8. Caregiver / Reporting

Purpose: Handle legal reporting via caregiver/advocate.

Questions (to caregiver):

“Have you reported this incident to the authorities?”

“Would you like assistance filing a report?”

“Do you want forensic samples collected for evidence?”

“Can we follow up for medical care and results?”

Normal: Caregiver understands options and consents to next steps.
Abnormal: Caregiver coerces child, refuses reporting despite risk.

9. Closure and Support

Purpose: Ensure follow-up, safety, and psychological support.

Questions:

“Do you have any remaining pain or questions?”

“Would you like to talk to a counselor or support person?”

“Do you understand what will happen next?”

Normal: Child reassured, caregiver aware, follow-up plan clear.
Abnormal: Child disoriented, unsafe, or refuses support.

✅ Key Principles

Use neutral, non-leading language.

Maintain caregiver involvement for consent and support.

Observe nonverbal cues: body language, distress, avoidance.

Use dolls or visual aids to help children demonstrate abuse safely.

Document exact words of the child for forensic purposes.
