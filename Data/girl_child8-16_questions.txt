## **Section 2: Menstrual Health and Hygiene**

*Analogy:* “Every month, your body prepares and clears itself naturally—this is your period.”

**Educational Notes:**

* **Pads:** Soft, absorbent, change every 4–6 hours, wash hands before and after.
* **Tampons (~13–16 yrs):** Small cylinder inserted inside, safe if used correctly, change every 3–6 hours, good for sports/swimming.
* Wash hands and maintain daily hygiene; wear clean underwear.

**Tactful Questions:**
8. Have you started noticing monthly bleeding?
9. How regular are these changes, and how many days do they usually last?
10. Do you experience pain, heavy bleeding, or discomfort during periods?
11. Do you know how to use pads or tampons safely at school and home?
12. Have these changes ever caused you to miss school or activities?
13. Do you have any questions about managing periods safely and hygienically?

## **Section 4: Body Ownership and Personal Safety**

*Analogy:* “Your body is a treasure chest—it belongs only to you. Saying ‘No’ and leaving unsafe situations are the most effective ways to protect yourself.”

**Safety Education:**

* Saying **“No” clearly** is the first and most effective step if someone touches you or asks you to do something you don’t want.
* **Running away** from unsafe situations is natural and protective.
* Tell a trusted adult afterward (teacher, nurse, parent).
* Your body is **your personal right**, and no one can take it without consent.

**Ages 8–12:**
19. Do you know what kinds of touches are safe and unsafe?
20. Has anyone ever touched you in a way that made you uncomfortable?
21. If someone touches you in a way that makes you uncomfortable, what would you do first?
22. Would you feel comfortable saying “No”?
23. If you feel unsafe, where would you go, or whom would you tell?

**Ages 13–16:**
24. Do you know situations that could be risky for girls as they grow?
25. Have you ever felt pressured to do something you didn’t want to do?
26. How would you protect yourself from unsafe sexual contact or situations?
27. Are you aware of risks like infections or early pregnancy?
28. If someone, even a relative or trusted adult, touches you inappropriately, what would you do first?
29. Would you feel comfortable saying “No” and leaving the situation?
30. Who would you feel safe telling if something like this happens?

### **Guidelines for Administration**

1. Conduct **privately**, one-on-one, in a safe and supportive environment.
2. Use **educational analogies** before sensitive questions to normalize experiences.
3. Ensure questions are **tactfully indirect, age-appropriate, and legally compliant**.
4. Allow girls to **skip questions** if uncomfortable.
5. Observe **non-verbal cues**—hesitation or anxiety may indicate prior experiences.
6. Maintain **confidentiality** and follow **mandatory reporting protocols** if abuse or danger is disclosed.
